# BaseDare — Launch & Sales Pack

> First touch sells the **loop**, not the worldview. Worldview (Chaos / Peebear / dare economy) is second-touch — the magnet, one layer down.

## The 5-beat pitch (memorize this)
1. **Fund a real-world mission.**
2. **Someone shows up.**
3. **QR + GPS proves it.**
4. **They get paid in USDC.**
5. **The venue gets signal.**

That's BaseDare. If you can't say it in 5 beats, you're overselling.

## Venue outreach — the wedge (fastest revenue)

**Cold DM / message (short):**
> Hey [venue] — I run BaseDare. We drive *verified* foot traffic to slow windows: we route local creators to check in during a dead hour, prove they showed up with QR + GPS, and you get a receipt of exactly who came. First pilot is small ($500–$1.5k) and you only care about one number: verified check-ins through your door. Want me to run one for your slowest window this week?

**In-person (30 sec):**
> "What's your deadest 2-hour window?" → "I can put 20 verified people through it. You give one small perk, I prove every single arrival with QR + GPS, and you get a one-page receipt. $500 pilot. Worst case you learn your slow window's real ceiling."

**The ask:** one pilot, one window, one perk. Close the *test*, not a contract.

### Pilot one-pager (what the venue buys)
- **Offer:** $500–$1.5k pilot ("First Spark").
- **Venue gives:** 1 simple perk (free coffee, happy-hour unlock, house item).
- **BaseDare does:** routes local creators + QR/GPS proof of every arrival.
- **Output (the Spark Receipt):** verified check-ins, time window, proof clips, baseline vs result, "repeat / adjust / stop" call.
- **Why it's safe:** you pay for *proven arrivals*, not promises. No bots, no fake reach.

## Objection handling
- *"Is this gambling?"* → No. Missions are **skill/task-based** (show up, do a thing, prove it). No chance, no wagering. Backers fund specific missions; creators earn for completing them.
- *"Why crypto?"* → Instant, borderless payout + tamper-proof receipts. The user never has to care it's onchain; they just get paid.
- *"How do I know they really showed up?"* → That's the whole point: QR + GPS proof. You can't fake presence — that's what makes our receipts trustworthy (and what Instagram/Beli/Atly can't do).
- *"$BARE token?"* → Points are **proof reputation** today. No token promises. (Keep it this way — protects the brand.)

## What "launched" looks like (the bar)
One city · ~20 founding creators · ~5 venue pilots sold · the core loop (fund → show up → prove → pay) visibly spinning · every completion posted as a receipt. *Not* a national network.
