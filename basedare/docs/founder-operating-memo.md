# BaseDare Founder Operating Memo

## Purpose

Keep BaseDare financially honest while building something original.

This memo is the anti-delusion document.
Use it to decide:
- what to prioritize
- what to measure
- what not to count
- where the real money is

## The Core Truth

BaseDare is not just:
- a dare app
- a creator marketplace
- a fun consumer product

BaseDare is trying to become:
- a challenge economy
- a creator activation system
- a venue activation layer
- a proof and settlement infrastructure

That means the company should be run as a layered business, not a single-loop app.

## What We Are Optimizing For

### Short term
- prove users fund dares
- prove creators complete them
- prove verification works
- prove we can sell paid campaigns

### Medium term
- prove venues can use BaseDare to create measurable energy and foot traffic
- prove repeat campaign demand
- prove recurring venue revenue

### Long term
- make BaseDare the default challenge and proof layer for third parties

## The Revenue Stack

### 1. Consumer

Role:
- attention engine
- creator acquisition engine
- culture engine

Do not expect it to carry the whole business.

### 2. Campaigns

Role:
- primary cash engine

This is where BaseDare becomes financially serious.

### 3. Venues

Role:
- recurring local revenue engine
- real-world moat builder

### 4. Protocol

Role:
- long-term scale and software leverage

## What Not To Lie To Ourselves About

### 1. GMV is not revenue

Funded volume is not company revenue.
Refunded volume is definitely not company revenue.
Live pot or treasury balances are not company revenue.

### 2. Consumer hype is not a business model

A lot of users, clips, or viral moments do not automatically mean strong economics.

### 3. Manual review is expensive

If too many dares require human review, small bounties become economically ugly.

### 4. Venue value must be proven

Venues will not pay because the concept sounds cool.
They will pay when:
- traffic is measurable
- repeat visits increase
- creators actually show up
- activations feel worth it

## What To Focus On Relentlessly

### 1. Realized revenue per successful challenge

Track:
- funded GMV
- settled GMV
- completion rate
- company revenue per resolved dare

### 2. Campaign value density

Track:
- average campaign size
- setup fee revenue
- campaign rake revenue
- gross margin per campaign
- repeat campaign rate

### 3. Venue proof of utility

Track:
- check-ins per venue
- active venue sessions
- venue retention
- repeat visitor rate
- activation revenue per venue

### 4. Ops cost per payout

Track:
- how many human touches per successful payout
- how often payouts fail or need retry
- how much support load each dispute creates

## Strategic Rules

### Rule 1
Keep consumer chaos alive because it creates the brand.

### Rule 2
Use B2B campaigns to fund the company.

### Rule 3
Use venues to build recurring revenue and local moat.

### Rule 4
Treat QR and proof as trusted transition infrastructure, not novelty.

### Rule 5
Do not over-rotate into token speculation before the core business works.

### Rule 6
Do not confuse community treasury mechanics with company economics.

## The Practical Priorities

### Priority A: Cash
- close paid campaigns
- validate pricing
- improve campaign delivery quality

### Priority B: Trust
- keep proof and payout rails reliable
- reduce fraud and ambiguity
- reduce payout failure

### Priority C: Place
- prove venue check-ins matter
- prove venue pages feel alive
- prove activations can move people

### Priority D: Scale
- systematize what works
- later open it into API / infrastructure

## The Category To Build

Not:
- social app
- loyalty app
- influencer marketplace

Yes:
- verified challenge infrastructure

That is the category worth building.

## One-Line Internal Reminder

Consumer creates the story.
Campaigns create the money.
Venues create the moat.
Infrastructure creates the scale.
