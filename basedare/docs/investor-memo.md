# BaseDare Investor Memo

## One-Line Thesis

BaseDare is building the challenge layer for the internet and the physical world: a system where money, proof, creators, and places combine to turn attention into verified action.

## Problem

Creator marketing and UGC still suffer from the same old problems:
- brands pay for reach, not verified action
- venues struggle to turn attention into measurable traffic
- creators rely on passive content instead of high-intent challenge loops
- social media is optimized for scrolling, not participation

## Solution

BaseDare turns bounties into verified content and real-world action.

Users fund dares.
Creators complete them.
Proof gets reviewed.
The protocol handles settlement.
Venues and cities can become live challenge nodes through check-ins and memory.

This creates a new unit of online and offline engagement:
- funded
- competitive
- visible
- measurable
- harder to fake

## Why Now

- short-form behavior is mature
- creator monetization is fragmenting
- brands want stronger attribution and authenticity
- local businesses need new traffic formats
- onchain stablecoin rails and wallet UX are finally usable enough to support programmatic payouts

## Wedge

Start where chaos, creators, and place already matter:
- nightlife
- local activations
- creator communities
- brand-funded challenge campaigns

This gives BaseDare:
- stronger stories
- higher-intent participation
- clearer venue and campaign ROI

## Business Model

BaseDare should be built as a stacked revenue business:

1. consumer successful-payout take rate
2. brand and campaign fees
3. venue subscriptions and activations
4. long-term protocol and verification infrastructure revenue

The company should not rely on consumer fees alone.

## Why It Wins

BaseDare is not just a bounty board.

It combines:
- funding
- verification
- settlement
- place
- creator competition

That makes it stronger than:
- generic creator marketplaces
- generic loyalty apps
- generic map apps
- generic influencer software

## Moat

The moat is not the baseline map or the UI.
The moat is the system:

- verified proof flow
- venue memory graph
- challenge reputation
- secure QR check-ins
- creator and venue network effects
- operational rails for payout and review

## Revenue Potential

The biggest upside comes from three engines:

1. brand challenge OS
- verified creator activations
- measurable campaign output

2. venue OS
- check-ins
- memory
- perks
- activation packages

3. protocol layer
- APIs
- verification-as-a-service
- white-label rails

Consumer dares remain important as the narrative and acquisition layer.

## Risks

The main risks are:
- fraud and proof quality
- too much human ops per challenge
- weak venue ROI
- relying on consumer virality as if it equals revenue
- letting the product drift into generic social features

## What Success Looks Like

BaseDare becomes huge when it is no longer described as:
- "a website where people dare creators"

and starts being described as:
- "the infrastructure for verified creator challenges, venue activations, and measurable social missions"

## Ask Framing

The strongest ask is not just capital.
It is:
- pilot brand intros
- venue pilot intros
- creator cluster intros
- strategic operators who understand creator marketing, nightlife, and local commerce
