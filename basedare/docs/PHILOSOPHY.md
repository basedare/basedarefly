# BaseDare — Core Philosophy & Operating Principles

> The durable "why" behind BaseDare. Read this before building features, writing copy, spending treasury, or planning GTM. This file and `brain-vault/00-control/vision.md` are one canon and must remain aligned.

## ⭐ THE SETTLED CORE MODEL — read first; we keep drifting from this

**BaseDare is a remote, bounty-funded local discovery network that pays people to create verified place intelligence.** The unit is still a *dare* — a safe, structured real-world question with a payout. A buyer or treasury funds the question → a local contributor performs it → BaseDare verifies the evidence → the contributor is paid → the answer becomes timestamped place memory → the receipt sells or recruits the next action.

The company must work without the founder living in, visiting, or personally operating any target market. A city is an interchangeable deployment zone, not the business. Events and hosted routes are optional higher-proof products delivered only through properly authorized local partners.

**The proof ladder remains the pricing logic** — charge more as proof moves down:

1. **execution** — the contributor was at the place and completed the task
2. **publication** — a usable field note, photo, clip, correction, or route exists
3. **attention** — people consumed, saved, shared, or followed the result
4. **conversion** — an attributed person arrived or took the requested action
5. **qualified** — the person bought, redeemed, returned, or met another buyer-approved condition

Venue-less asynchronous discovery naturally starts on rungs 1–3. Rungs 4–5 require stronger evidence such as a venue or merchant co-sign, secure handshake, attributable transaction, booking, redemption, or another buyer-approved confirmation. Do not pretend a GPS-backed field note proves a purchase.

**Verification is the product bet and the cost center.** BaseDare does not yet own a verification moat; it is building one. The business lives or dies on whether it can cheaply verify paid strangers without a venue QR or founder present. **Do not scale venue-less reward spend until submission-time proximity, freshness, uniqueness, rights, review cost, fraud rate, and dispute handling have survived real paid contributors.**

BaseDare proofs outcomes; it does not insure or guarantee them. Funds may be escrowed and settled by the protocol, but BaseDare does not promise incrementality, guaranteed traffic, or guaranteed commercial results.

**Every paid dare must create a durable asset:** verified presence · structured local information · reusable media or a field note with sufficient rights · a serial receipt · contributor reputation · a referral or discovery path. If a payout creates only a disposable post, it is marketing spend without a network effect.

**First SKU = one $2,500 Verified Field Sprint.** The fixed invoice is a $2,000 managed-service fee plus a separately tracked $500 creator pool. Four contributors are funded at $125 gross each; an accepted result pays $120 net after the V2 4% settlement fee. Use a friendly design partner at cost or free only to manufacture the first honest receipt and measure delivery. Sprint #1 proves delivery and verification economics; Sprint #2 is the first real paid sale.

**Financial rails are separate and canonical.** Personal/community dares are self-serve: 96% completer, 4% BaseDare, 0% referral, and no Live Pot entitlement. The 4% is settlement/liquidity revenue, not managed-service revenue. Business service fees are invoiced off-contract; the V2 contract carries only creator reward funding. `docs/FINANCIAL_CANON.md` and `lib/financial-canon.ts` are the pricing authority. A future 25% all-in self-serve business rail is parked until delivery is automated and proven.

**Service → asset is non-negotiable.** A Field Sprint is a service wedge, not the destination. Every sprint must reuse templates, improve verification, add place records, strengthen contributor reputation, and create re-verification demand. Local information decays; every result carries an `as of` date and a refresh expectation. Freshness decay is both a product liability to communicate honestly and the bridge to recurring **City Signal** revenue.

**The contributor funnel is money-first:** see a specific paid mission → claim → prove → get paid → reputation activates → receive the next mission. Do not require a cold contributor to build an identity and wait in an empty marketplace before touching value. The wallet-first claim and proximity-evidence implementation exists locally; it is not production proof until migrations, deployment, and a signed end-to-end smoke are complete.

**Non-negotiable safety constraint:** dare briefs come from a constrained, pre-approved menu; payout never scales with danger, humiliation, harassment, drinking pressure, trespass, or escalation-for-views. Good discovery templates include **Verify**, **Correct**, **Taste**, **Hunt**, **Route**, and **Story** dares. The useful and safe version is the only version that pays.

**⚠️ ANTI-DRIFT.** BaseDare is not a generic map, travel blog, influencer agency, events company requiring founder presence, passive check-in farm, token-farming scheme, or anonymous local operating business hidden behind crypto. The core is the dare + verification + place memory + receipt. Everything else must strengthen that loop.

## What BaseDare is (one sentence)

**Proof-backed discovery for the physical world:** fund useful real-world questions, reward verified answers, attach them to places, and let the resulting memory compound.

## The category we're in

BaseDare sits between distributed field research, local discovery, creator work, and proof-of-presence infrastructure. Consumer demand is still emotional — discovery, confidence, adventure, belonging — while the buyer pays for fresh evidence, reusable content, verified action, or an increasingly reliable view of the physical world.

## The 8 principles

1. **Proof, not claims.** GPS, time, media, deduplication, secure handshakes, corroboration, and reputation form a proof ladder. None alone is the moat. The moat being built is the accumulated verified place history, contributor reliability, fraud knowledge, buyer demand, and re-verification loop.

2. **The map is the feed.** No global engagement timeline. Open a place to see current signal, history, open questions, and who verified it. Geographic density matters more than global signup count.

3. **Lead with usefulness; back it with verified + paid.** Explorers want a better decision now. Contributors want a clear paid task. Buyers want evidence they can reuse. Crypto is settlement infrastructure, not the first-touch pitch.

4. **Contributors earn; buyers fund.** BaseDare treasury seeds the first supply. Ecosystem grants can fund the verification and market-selection experiment. Brands, travel products, hostels, mobility providers, wallets, local business groups, venues, and later tourism organizations fund useful questions.

5. **One question, one bounded batch, one receipt.** Concentrate each experiment. Test several candidate markets with the same rules, then direct density into the winner. Do not confuse location independence with spreading rewards everywhere.

6. **Best part is no part.** Subtraction over addition. Reuse the nearby-dare, Local Signals, Sentinel, proof, payout, place-memory, receipt, and reputation rails. Build only what blocks a real sprint or makes verified delivery materially cheaper.

7. **Reputation now, token later.** Signal Points represent proof-gated reliability. No promised `$BARE` value, speculative farming incentive, or payout for hype.

8. **Services must compound into infrastructure.** Bespoke fieldwork is allowed only when it produces reusable templates, records, verification learnings, contributor history, refresh demand, and a path to recurring or software revenue.

## The machine

**Someone funds a useful real-world question. A contributor shows up. BaseDare verifies the answer. The contributor gets paid. The place remembers. Everyone gets a receipt.**

The founder governs the mechanism and treasury; the founder does not host the experience. The app is the discovery and receipt surface. The verification system and place-memory graph are the infrastructure being built.

## The roles

- **Explorers** use the map and receipts to decide where to go or what to trust.
- **Contributors / proof creators** complete paid discovery dares and build portable reputation.
- **Buyers / sponsors** fund useful physical-world questions and receive verified deliverables.
- **Places / venues** may claim records, correct information, add secure handshakes, co-sign higher proof, or fund their own missions. They are not required for the first venue-less discovery rung.
- **Hosts / local operators** are optional partners for gatherings, routes, or events. BaseDare does not require them for asynchronous fieldwork.
- **BaseDare** structures the question, verifies the evidence, settles the payout, records the memory, and produces the receipt.

## Wedge vs. machine

The first wedge is **verified fieldwork before audience**. A buyer can purchase a current, evidence-backed answer without BaseDare already owning consumer distribution. The first design partner creates the case-study receipt; the first paid sponsor funds the next Sprint.

The machine travels wherever paid contributors can answer useful place questions remotely. Candidate markets compete on proof cost, completion, repeat contributors, fraud, organic actions, density, receipt sharing, and buyer pull. Siargao is one optional market, not a required beachhead.

## Buyer ladder

1. **BaseDare treasury** funds a tiny design-partner proof batch.
2. **Base / Coinbase / Optimism-style ecosystem funding** is pursued to underwrite verifiable onchain real-world activity and the bounded market tournament.
3. **Travel and consumer sponsors** buy Verified Field Sprints and Discovery Drops.
4. **Recurring City Signal buyers** pay for freshness, re-verification, and continuously updated place intelligence.
5. **Places and venues** fund missions or higher-proof conversion/qualified outcomes when they see useful demand.
6. **Tourism organizations, travel platforms, map products, and AI systems** buy campaigns, data, or verification infrastructure only after the dataset is dense and credible.

Do not skip from founder treasury to a grand tourism-platform story. Do not claim a venue partnership without explicit authorization.

## Roadmap — gated by evidence, not calendar

1. **Harden the sellable proof path** — cold-wallet claim, venue-less proximity proof, explicit sponsor-usable media/data rights.
2. **Design-partner Sprint** — run one small remote batch at cost/free and measure verification cost, fraud, rejection, freshness, and contributor behavior.
3. **Ecosystem grant + bounded market tournament** — seek non-founder funding, apply common rules across candidate markets, and concentrate in the winner.
4. **Paid Verified Field Sprint** — sell the second batch as a deliverable, not as reach or guaranteed traffic.
5. **Recurring City Signal** — re-verify decaying facts and sell freshness rather than one-off reports.
6. **Higher proof rungs** — add venue/merchant co-signs, redemptions, purchases, bookings, and attributed actions.
7. **Verified place graph infrastructure** — data/API/white-label only after reliable density and repeat buyers exist.

### Partial or missing — blocks a paid commercial launch

1. **Production proof is still required.** Run the required migrations before code, then complete a real signed-wallet claim → upload → proximity decision → review/payout/refund → receipt smoke. Local tests are not production evidence.
2. **Sponsor-usable media rights are partial.** Current Terms grant display and BaseDare promotional rights, but the upload flow does not capture explicit campaign-specific commercial reuse/sublicensing consent for sponsor deliverables. Do not sell that right yet.
3. **Managed delivery remains manual.** Contributor routing, batch funding, paid-reviewer accounting, invoice confirmation, and receipt assembly may be operated manually for the design-partner Sprint, but must stay within the canonical $650 direct-cost and 10-hour ceilings.
4. **V2 mainnet is not deployed.** The financial canon is locked, but human-signed deployment and production cutover remain separate release work governed by `docs/runbooks/mainnet-cutover.md`.

## Build discipline

- **Working beats perfect.** Fix the narrow proof path before adding new surfaces.
- **Verify before shipping.** Run real signed-wallet, geolocation, proof, review, payout, rights, and receipt paths end to end.
- **Run water through the pipes.** A database record is not proof that a stranger can claim, complete, and get paid remotely.
- **Separate real from sample data.** Never present seeded/test records as traction, liquidity, or customer proof.
- **Two agents, one repo.** Respect file ownership and update coordination notes after shipping.

## Operating loops

1. **Launch smoke loop** — env, chain, auth, proof, payout, and public-data integrity.
2. **Money-first contributor loop** — specific paid mission → cold wallet claim → proof → payout → identity/reputation activation.
3. **Venue-less verification loop** — submission location + time + media + uniqueness + radius + risk routing.
4. **Local Signals loop** — useful structured field answers move through review into discovery surfaces.
5. **Sentinel loop** — uncertain or higher-risk proofs reach a remote reviewer with clear evidence and outcomes.
6. **Place-memory loop** — accepted proof becomes timestamped, attributable, re-verifiable memory.
7. **Receipt loop** — every real batch produces an honest deliverable with accepted, rejected, cost, freshness, and rights status.
8. **Design-partner loop** — Sprint #1 creates the case study and verification-cost baseline.
9. **Grant loop** — seek ecosystem funding for verifiable onchain real-world activity without pretending grant revenue is customer PMF.
10. **Paid Sprint loop** — Sprint #2 is paid and delivered at known gross margin.
11. **Freshness loop** — decaying place facts create scheduled re-verification and recurring City Signal value.
12. **Learning loop** — every claim, rejection, fraud attempt, dispute, buyer objection, and renewal changes the next batch.

## Hard gates

- **No scaled venue-less treasury spend until proximity verification and fraud/review economics are proven with paid strangers.**
- **No sponsor promise of reusable media/data until the necessary contributor rights are explicitly captured.**
- **No new product surface unless it unblocks sellable proof, reduces verification cost, or improves a loop that has produced real evidence.**
