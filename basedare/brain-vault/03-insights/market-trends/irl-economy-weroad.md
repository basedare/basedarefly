# The IRL economy is funded & hot — BaseDare is the verified+paid version

## Statement
The "IRL economy" (young people paying for offline experiences + human connection, not screens) is a real, fundable, accelerating category. WeRoad — group travel for solo travelers — raised **$58M led by Airbnb** (2026), ~$100M total, on **€130M revenue / 100k travelers (2025)**. Their wedge: "young adults struggle to meet people." BaseDare is in this exact category, with a stronger position: **verified (proof-of-presence) + paid (you earn, not pay) + onchain.**

## Evidence
- TechCrunch: "Airbnb-backed WeRoad raises $58M to take its group travel platform to the US" (2026-05-27).
- Skift / Vestbee / PhocusWire corroborate: Airbnb led, 10% stake + board seat; CEO hired to run Airbnb hotels.
- WeRoad US playbook: "focus on a small number of cities first, **beginning with Austin**, recruit **group leaders**, organize **local events**, build **community partnerships** before expanding." (≈ BaseDare's Siargao beachhead playbook.)
- Source seen via IG @business_bagel post framing the "IRL economy" trend.

## Applies To
- **Positioning/fundraising:** "Airbnb just put $58M into a peer (WeRoad); BaseDare is the verified + paid version of the IRL economy."
- **GTM:** our one-city beachhead + founding-creator recruiting + local events is the *same* playbook a ~$100M company uses → keep doing it; add a real Siargao founding-creator meetup ("WeMeet"-style).
- **Copy:** WeRoad sells connection/adventure/belonging; BaseDare leans too transactional ("wild shit / get paid"). Add the emotional hook.
- **Roadmap:** group missions / **Dare Routes** (curated, hosted, 8–15 person, social) is the fundable shape (v1.1, after the single loop is proven).

## Behavioral Rule
1. Frame BaseDare in the "IRL economy" category in pitches/decks; cite the WeRoad raise as the tailwind.
2. Consumer-facing copy leads with **connection / adventure / belonging**; "verified + paid" is the moat underneath (not the headline).
3. Keep the beachhead + founding-creator + local-event motion — it's validated.
4. Treat **Dare Routes / group missions** as confirmed direction, sequenced after one proven paid loop.
