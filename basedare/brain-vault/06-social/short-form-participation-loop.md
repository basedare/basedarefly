# Short-Form Participation Loop

## Decision

TikTok and Instagram are not awareness-only channels for BaseDare. They are the
narrated entrance to the playable map.

Every short-form episode should make one viewer feel personally understood, show one
real and reachable possibility, and direct them into one useful map action. Activity on
the map then creates the proof, place memory, receipt, or social outcome that becomes
the next episode.

`seed a live map state -> capture a real artifact -> narrate one story -> link one action
-> someone participates -> the place remembers -> the receipt becomes the next story`

A post that earns attention but sends nobody into a useful BaseDare action is creative
research, not successful acquisition.

## Initial audience problem

Start narrower than "travel discovery" or "social map":

> I am somewhere new, I do not know many people, and I want something worthwhile to do
> in the next two hours.

Siargao can supply the first episodes, but this is a portable audience problem rather
than a location-dependent company thesis.

## Locked signature series

### 1. PeeBear's Next Two Hours

- **Pillar:** Join in
- **Viewer tension:** "What should I do now, especially if I am solo?"
- **Story:** PeeBear identifies one best live option and no more than two quieter
  alternatives.
- **Artifact:** a current map state, real Drop/Spark/Dare, honest going count, or recent
  place signal.
- **CTA:** open the narrowest live BaseDare surface that supports the featured action.

PeeBear is a guide and narrator, not decoration. He gets one useful line, points toward
an action, and gets out of the way.

### 2. Rumor or Real?

- **Pillar:** Secret map or Receipts
- **Viewer tension:** "People keep mentioning this place. Is it actually real or useful?"
- **Story:** show an approximate, safety-preserving rumor; invite or follow one bounded
  verification mission; reveal only what the resulting evidence supports.
- **Artifact:** an approved Local Signal, mission, evidence outcome, place-memory update,
  or receipt.
- **CTA:** investigate the live mission or open the updated place.

Never expose a sensitive exact location, manufacture a rumor, or call uncertain evidence
verified for the sake of the reveal.

### 3. Worth It Right Now?

- **Pillar:** Buyer stories or Receipts
- **Viewer tension:** "Is this place actually worth going to now, not six months ago?"
- **Story:** a buyer or community asks one bounded current question; contributors answer;
  BaseDare shows the timestamped outcome and its limits.
- **Artifact:** a real funded mission, accepted/rejected evidence summary, current place
  signal, or buyer receipt.
- **CTA:** open the place, complete the next field check, or view the buyer mission.

This format demonstrates the sellable Field Sprint without promising foot traffic,
incrementality, sales, or a guaranteed commercial result.

## Episode anatomy

Target 15-25 seconds unless the artifact needs more context:

1. **0-2 seconds — personal hook:** name the viewer's immediate situation.
2. **2-5 seconds — open loop:** reveal that the map knows or needs one useful thing.
3. **5-12 seconds — playable choice:** show one hero action and at most two alternatives.
4. **12-18 seconds — proof:** show the real map state, field artifact, or receipt.
5. **18-22 seconds — one CTA:** send the viewer to one live BaseDare destination.

Lead with the viewer, not the feature. For example:

- not "We launched Adventure Mode"; use "You have two free hours and know nobody."
- not "The map has 24 venues"; use "Here is the place worth checking tonight."
- not "Earn USDC on BaseDare"; use a real funded mission and describe exactly what it asks.

## Format research workflow

Before each 30-day cycle, collect 30-50 strong short-form references from the active
niche. Record:

- audience problem
- first frame and first spoken line
- curiosity gap
- emotional trigger
- story beats and duration
- reveal or proof device
- CTA
- comments that reveal why people cared

Reuse the underlying psychology and structure. Do not copy a creator's distinctive
script, footage, visual identity, or unverified claim.

## Thirty-day test

Run twelve artifact-led episodes: four variants of each signature series, with no more
than three total posts per week. If the required artifact or reuse rights do not exist,
publish fewer posts and extend the test. Never fill the calendar with fabricated proof.

Before publishing an episode:

- the featured action is still live and understandable
- the linked map area is not empty or misleading
- any alternatives shown are real and current
- the asset is owned or has explicit recorded reuse consent
- every number has been checked against the live product that day
- the CTA uses a tracked deep link to the narrowest supported destination

Creator-partner episodes should use a server-issued tracked redirect once the Journey
rail is live. Browser-local UTMs are a temporary measurement aid, not sufficient proof
of creator-to-completion attribution. Cross-browser persistence, lock-on-start,
passwordless Mission Pass handoff, duplicate-intent merging, and the separate
path-attributed-completion / creator-mission-participation reporting categories are
specified in `../05-playbooks/creator-attribution-journey.md`. The creator link may open
anonymous map discovery inside a social webview; wallet execution resumes only from the
Mission Pass in an ordinary browser.

Fable may template the motion system, captions, map zooms, PeeBear entrance, and receipt
reveal. It may not manufacture a field result, participant reaction, going count, payout,
or buyer outcome.

## Scorecard

**North star: qualified map actions per 1,000 short-form views.**

A qualified map action is attributable traffic that opens the featured place, mission,
Spark, Drop, or Route and continues into an intentional action such as save, join, claim,
or proof. Until the full event funnel is instrumented, use attributable BaseDare clicks
plus logged downstream claims, joins, missions, and buyer conversations as the honest
proxy.

Do not treat a creator performance path as fully attributable until it is tied to a
canonical server-side ActionIntent, participant binding and verified outcome. A verified
completion of a creator-authored or creator-narrated mission without the preserved path
is **creator-mission participation**, not payout-grade path attribution. Until the full
rail exists, label results as click/session-level or mission-participation evidence only.

Supporting signals:

- 3-second and 6-second hold where the platform exposes them
- completion and rewatch
- shares and saves
- profile visits
- click-through to BaseDare
- action opens, starts, completions, and participant return

Decision rules after at least three honest variants:

- **REPEAT:** the format earns useful attention and produces downstream map action.
- **ITERATE:** it earns views/shares but loses people between the post and the map.
- **KILL:** it repeatedly produces neither meaningful attention nor a downstream action.

Follower growth is welcome, but it is not the operating objective.

## Approval and safety boundary

Research, format analysis, drafting, templating, and scorecard preparation may be
automatic. Public posting, replies, DMs, partnerships, pricing, payout language, and
external promises remain founder-reviewed and manually executed under
`approval-queue.md`.
