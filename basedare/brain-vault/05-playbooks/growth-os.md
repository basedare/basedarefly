# BaseDare Growth OS

## Purpose

BaseDare grows when verified field activity becomes a buyer receipt and that receipt creates another funded decision.

The operating loop is:

`qualified conversation → bounded buyer question → funded Sprint → routed contributors → accepted evidence → durable receipt → repeat / adjust / stop decision`

The Growth OS exists to run that loop every day without confusing motion with progress. It is not a social dashboard, a spam engine, a generic CRM, or a replacement for the existing proof, settlement, Sprint, attribution, and place-memory ledgers.

## The weekly boss fight

The default weekly objective is:

> Deliver one conservative Verified Field Sprint receipt and obtain one explicit buyer decision.

The locked commercial SKU remains:

- `$2,000` managed service.
- `$500` creator reward pool.
- Four independent missions funded at `$125` gross each.
- The existing 4% settlement fee is liquidity/settlement economics, not the managed-service fee.

A named design-partner exception may be pro bono, but it must still display the real price, reward cost, design-partner reason, evidence limitations, and the conversion question for Sprint #2. Free work without a decision owner or repeat ask is not a pilot; it is unbounded services work.

## What earns Growth score

Growth score is an internal rolling seven-day output index. It is not money, reputation, a token, compensation, or a public leaderboard.

| Verified state | Points | Why it counts |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Sprint funding confirmed | 100 | A real buyer/design-partner scope reached funded execution. |
| Sprint receipt completed | 200 | Four accepted outcomes became a durable buyer artifact. |
| Buyer records REPEAT | 150 | The receipt produced explicit repeat intent. This is not booked revenue. |
| Mission settled | 20 | A mission passed the existing evidence and settlement rails. |
| Place record approved | 8 | The batch compounded verified place intelligence. |
| Field Station arrival verified | 12 | Physical acquisition reached the secure arrival boundary. |
| Venue check-in confirmed | 2 | Presence signal exists, without implying a purchase. |

The following always earn zero:

- Messages sent.
- Followers, impressions, likes, views, and raw scans.
- Directions opened, Mission Passes saved, or pages viewed.
- Drafts, meetings without a next decision, and dashboards opened.
- Unaccepted proof, invented buyer outcomes, or unconfirmed revenue.

Negative and inconclusive answers count as valid delivery when the evidence is accepted. The system rewards truthful completion, not pleasing answers.

## Daily founder loop

Target: roughly four focused hours. If time is constrained, do the blocks in order.

### 1. Read the field — 20 minutes

- Open `/admin/growth-os`.
- Read the top three quests in order.
- Clear proof-review or payout risk before creating more demand.
- Name the single bottleneck: buyer, supply, verification, settlement, receipt, repeat, or distribution.
- Do not create a new feature to avoid the bottleneck.

Output: one written command for the day.

### 2. Buyer pipeline — 90 minutes

Growth administrator prepares:

- 10 researched venue, travel, hospitality, tourism, eSIM, coworking, hostel, or local-brand targets.
- Five genuinely personal outreach drafts grounded in a real place question.
- Three follow-ups on warm replies or delivered receipts.
- A clean owner, status, objection, evidence, and next date for every active lead.

Founder does:

- At least one real buyer conversation.
- Ask: “What decision about this place are you currently making with weak or stale information?”
- Reduce the answer to one observable, time-bounded question.
- End with one next decision: scope review, invoice, question revision, or stop.

Output: qualified replies and scoped buyer questions, not an outbound count.

### 3. Contributor bench — 45 minutes

For every funded or likely mission, record:

- Area and transport radius.
- Availability and time window.
- Wallet and claim readiness.
- Camera/media capability.
- Conflict with the buyer or destination.
- Reliability based on accepted proof—not follower count.

Maintain at least four distinct route-ready contributors in the active area. Do not promise work before funding or let a contributor approve their own proof.

Output: a bounded supply slate for real demand.

### 4. Sprint delivery — 60 minutes

- Remove the highest-leverage blocker on the active Sprint.
- Ensure four independent mission contracts and distinct contributors.
- Escalate unclear briefs before fieldwork.
- Keep negative/inconclusive outcomes payable when the evidence satisfies the contract.
- Preserve the separation between growth, verifier, and payout roles.

Output: funded work advances one authoritative lifecycle state.

### 5. Distribution health — 30 minutes

- Check two permissioned Field Stations or two digital creator entry points.
- Scan the QR/link on a real phone.
- Confirm the promise has enough nearby inventory.
- Check the handoff, target open, Mission Pass, and directions path.
- Remove or reroute stale promises immediately.

Output: healthy entry points, never raw scan volume.

### 6. Log and learn — 15 minutes

Record:

- What changed in the real pipeline.
- Buyer words and objections.
- Contributor blockers.
- Evidence/verification friction.
- One format or tactic to REPEAT, ITERATE, or KILL.
- Every lead’s next owner and date.

Output: tomorrow starts from memory instead of archaeology.

## Weekly rhythm

### Monday — choose the bottleneck

- Review rolling Growth score and the Founder Scoreboard.
- Select one buyer segment and one question family.
- Select one active area with enough contributor density.
- Set the boss-fight target: one receipt and one buyer decision.

### Tuesday — create conversations

- Founder runs discovery calls.
- Growth administrator completes research, personal drafts, and warm follow-ups.
- Reject vague “exposure” briefs; translate them into observable questions.

### Wednesday — route the field

- Confirm four independent contributors.
- Confirm logistics, freshness windows, mission comprehension, and proof route.
- Check Field Station inventory if physical acquisition is part of the Sprint.

### Thursday — close evidence

- Verifier clears evidence without sales pressure.
- Ops reconciles settlement and payout states.
- Receipt limitations, privacy-safe evidence, and rights are checked.

### Friday — deliver and ask

- Deliver the receipt in a short buyer call.
- Ask the buyer to choose REPEAT, ADJUST, ASK, or STOP.
- A repeat is not revenue until the next scope and funding are confirmed.
- Publish or schedule a factual story only when rights and human approval are clear.

### Weekend — strengthen density

- Field Station keeper checks permission, condition, tampering, and QR behavior.
- Contributor router refreshes availability.
- Place-memory gaps found during the week become bounded future questions—not unpaid cold-user labor.

## Hiring order

Do not hire a generic “Head of Growth” first. Hire for the bottleneck in this order.

### 1. Growth Administrator

Best first hire once the founder can close but cannot maintain pipeline discipline.

Responsibilities:

- Research qualified buyers and venue partners.
- Prepare personal outreach drafts and evidence-led follow-ups.
- Maintain owners, statuses, objections, next actions, and dates.
- Schedule calls and prepare buyer briefs.
- Convert completed receipts into repeat/adjust decision requests.

Daily quota:

- 10 researched qualified targets.
- Five personal drafts.
- Three warm follow-ups.
- Zero active leads without a next owner/date.

What they may not do:

- Change pricing or scope.
- Promise outcomes, traffic, purchases, or exclusivity.
- Sign contracts or confirm funding.
- Accept proof, settle payouts, or access wallets.
- Receive the global BaseDare admin secret.

Evaluation after 30 days:

- Qualified reply rate.
- Meetings with real decision-makers.
- Buyer questions scoped.
- Sprints funded.
- Receipt-to-next-decision time.

Do not evaluate them primarily on outbound volume.

### 2. Contributor Router

Hire when funded work or likely demand is waiting on reliable supply.

Responsibilities:

- Recruit and screen local contributors.
- Maintain availability and capability.
- Match people to funded missions.
- Explain briefs and follow up on submissions.
- Escalate safety, conflicts, and delivery blockers.

They cannot approve evidence, change rewards, or trigger payouts.

### 3. Field Station Keeper / Local Partner

Use only through explicit, lawful, permissioned local arrangements.

Responsibilities:

- Secure placement permission.
- Check QR condition and tampering.
- Test links on real devices.
- Report whether the local promise remains truthful.

They are not concealed employees, nominee operators, or venue representatives unless separately authorized.

### 4. Verifier / Ops

Keep independent from growth incentives.

Responsibilities:

- Review evidence.
- Run Sentinel/manual escalation.
- Reconcile settlement and payout.
- Check receipt truth, limitations, privacy, and rights.

Their quality must never be judged by whether a buyer liked the answer.

## Access model

Until scoped staff authentication exists:

- Founder opens Growth OS and assigns bounded work packs.
- Staff use the specific operational tools and documents they need.
- Never share `ADMIN_SECRET`, referee keys, payout credentials, or wallet access.
- External sends, pricing, money, evidence decisions, and public claims remain explicit human gates.

Scoped Growth Administrator accounts can be built after the first hire proves that shared access—not pipeline execution—is the bottleneck.

## Hiring budget guidance

Keep this as a small, measurable trial rather than a permanent team bet:

- Growth Administrator: 30-day paid trial with a base retainer and a bonus only on funded Sprints or completed buyer decisions—not messages sent.
- Contributor Router: part-time or per active Sprint until mission volume is consistent.
- Field Station Keeper: per permissioned route/check or a small lawful local retainer; avoid informal hidden operating arrangements.
- Verifier / Ops: pay for independent review coverage and settlement reliability, not approval rate.

Do not add all four roles before one repeatable buyer segment and question family exist.

## Stop conditions

Pause expansion and fix the system when:

- Review or payout queues are growing.
- A poster/link promises inventory that is not present.
- Free pilots do not produce explicit next decisions.
- Buyers ask for generic content instead of an observable question.
- Contributors are being routed before funding or without safe briefs.
- Growth pressure is influencing proof decisions.
- The asset is not compounding into place memory and receipts.
