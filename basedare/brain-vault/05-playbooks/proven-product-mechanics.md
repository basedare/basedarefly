# Proven Product Mechanics

## Decision

BaseDare will adapt five proven mechanics from geocaching, location games, and distributed fieldwork without copying their generic feeds, engagement loops, or closed data models.

`approved question → verified observation → place health → playable route → earned endorsement → buyer next action`

These are one compounding system, not five unrelated features. Each must strengthen the dare, verification, place-memory, and receipt loop.

## 1. Approved Mission Kits and preflight compiler

Managed Field Truth work starts from an approved Mission Kit, not an unconstrained prompt. A kit freezes the observable question type, structured answer, truthful negative path, evidence, freshness, field time, reward, safety, privacy, rejection reasons, and recheck rule.

The first kits are **Open now, Offer available, Current price, Event active, Accessible now, and Crowd level**. Preflight rejects subjective, unsafe, unbounded, and freshness-incompatible briefs before funding. Every Outcome Contract stores its complete versioned kit snapshot so later edits cannot rewrite the funded job. A custom buyer request may be translated into an approved kit; it may not silently bypass the kit.

## 2. Maintenance outcomes and place health

Accepted observations resolve to **Confirmed, Changed, Could not verify, Closed or moved, Unsafe or inaccessible, or Needs recheck**. Public place health is **Fresh, Aging, Needs recheck, Disputed, or Retired**.

Health is derived from accepted append-only observations, never venue marketing. Aging or conflicting memory may propose a bounded re-verification mission, but may not autonomously spend money.

## 3. Playable discovery routes

Routes use three to five real stops, ordered or free play, one useful lore unlock per stop, directions to the next public access point, Mission Pass continuity, and a durable route receipt. Only fresh, non-disputed stops can be published. BaseDare owns story and progression; an external map provider handles navigation. Hosted or paid routes require proper local authorization.

## 4. Scarce verified endorsements

BaseDare will not add generic five-star ratings. **Worth a Detour** is earned through verified contribution and can be spent only on a place the person visited or helped verify. It is scarce, attached to verified history, separate from venue authority, retractable by its author, suppressible under moderation, and displayed with count and freshness rather than as universal truth.

## 5. Receipt next-action engine

Every completed Sprint receipt recommends one conservative decision: **recheck after freshness, correct the record, test a different time, collect a second bounded sample, replace an unavailable offer, investigate a conflict, or take no action**.

The engine uses outcome distribution, evidence quality, freshness, and conflicts. Negative and inconclusive answers remain useful results. It never converts scans, directions, attention, or one observation into a claim of foot traffic or commercial success.

## Sequence and gates

1. Mission Kits and preflight in the existing Sprint Runner.
2. Place health and safe human-approved recheck proposals.
3. Route object and health gate; pilot one 3-stop route.
4. One scarce earned endorsement with strict eligibility.
5. One conservative recommendation on the buyer receipt.

No autonomous funding, generic ratings, open-ended mission generation, or route marketplace is authorized. Every slice must pass Outcome Contract, proof, payout, privacy, RLS, and receipt gates before `main`.

## Measures

- funded work compiled from approved kits
- briefs corrected or rejected before money moves
- accepted observations with refresh dates
- aged or disputed records successfully reverified
- route start-to-completion and Mission Pass save rate
- endorsements per qualified participant and retraction/report rate
- buyer next-action acceptance and paid repeat rate
