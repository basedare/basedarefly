# First-Node Conversion Loop

Status: alpha operating rail

Owner: BaseDare operator

North star: turn one truthful venue question into one funded mission, one accepted answer, and one honest repeat-or-stop decision.

## The loop

`unsigned venue → informed response → scoped close room → human-approved payment → funded Dare/Campaign → accepted evidence → buyer receipt → repeat, adjust, or stop`

This is the smallest buyer-validation loop. It is not Scout Phase 2, automated outreach, a generic CRM, or a claim that BaseDare already has a venue partnership.

## Evidence vocabulary

Use these labels exactly:

- **VERIFIED** — only evidence accepted through the existing review rail.
- **PUBLIC SIGNAL** — public venue identity, mapped context, and other non-verified inputs.
- **PILOT PREVIEW — NOT YET FUNDED** — a proposed question or route. It is not a receipt, certificate, partnership, launch, or result.

Never turn page views, directions opens, messages, proposals, or public listings into arrivals, purchases, foot traffic, or verified outcomes.

## Venue response boundary

The decision brief must work without an account or wallet. It offers four explicit responses:

1. Request a scoped pilot.
2. Correct the brief.
3. Ask a question.
4. Decline.

Capture the responder's role, authority, contact channel, requested correction/question, timing, and preferred payment path only where needed. A correction or decline must remain low-friction. A pilot request opens the existing Activation Intake and secure Close Room; it does not approve, fund, or launch anything.

## Approval and money rails

- Scope approval may move an intake to `QUALIFIED` only.
- Payment confirmation remains a separate human-operated state transition.
- A paid Campaign and funded Dare remain the only execution rail.
- Proof review, payout leases, settlement, appeals, and the locked financial canon remain unchanged.
- No automated creator bonus or Scout economics are introduced here.

## Honest buyer receipt

A buyer receipt states:

- the exact question;
- the answer as of a timestamp;
- evidence method and acceptance state;
- creator and payout state;
- what is not proven;
- rights status; and
- the next decision: repeat, adjust, or stop.

Before a linked funded campaign exists, call the artifact a **pilot decision record**, label it **PILOT PREVIEW — NOT YET FUNDED**, and do not call it a result receipt.

Sponsor commercial-reuse rights are not granted by this loop. Existing BaseDare display rights remain separate; explicit sponsor reuse consent is still Phase 3 and requires human legal review.

## Handoff and measurement

Decision briefs may be handed off through native share, WhatsApp, Instagram-copy, email, print, or QR. The channel and time are logged. The QR is a handoff link only; it cannot prove presence or approve a pilot.

Measure:

`brief opened → channel handoff → structured response → scope approved → payment confirmed → mission launched → evidence accepted → payout settled → repeat/adjust/stop`

Do not count generic shares or unstructured messages as approval.

## Stop conditions

Stop or correct the loop when:

- an authorized venue person disputes the place or question;
- the buyer cannot name an observable decision;
- payment or reward pool is not confirmed;
- accepted evidence cannot answer the question;
- the report implies traffic, purchases, partnership, or media rights that were not proven; or
- the second bounded test does not earn a repeat decision.
