# First Customer + Contributor Launch

## Objective

Close one real design-partner Verified Field Sprint, deliver four independently verified answers, publish one conservative receipt, and turn the buyer's explicit `REPEAT` or `ADJUST` decision into the first full-price follow-on Sprint.

This is a controlled launch, not a broad public launch. BaseDare is ready to recruit the cell and qualify the buyer while the production migration blocker is being closed. Do not take live Sprint funds or promise a start date until the migration history and production-like end-to-end smoke test pass.

## The single offer

**Verified Field Sprint — $2,500 total**

- $2,000 managed service
- $500 gross contributor reward pool
- one bounded real-world question
- four independent $125 missions
- $120 net payout per accepted, settled contribution; $5 settlement fee
- 7–10 day expected delivery window
- evidence review, honest outcome distribution, timestamped receipt, and repeat/adjust/ask/stop decision
- no guaranteed positive answer, reach, traffic, sales, publication, or commercial media reuse

Sprint #1 may use the recorded design-partner exception: waive some or all of the $2,000 service fee, but keep the full $500 reward pool funded. Always show the normal $2,500 price and label the exception as acquisition cost—not a discount price.

## Do not conflate the three groups

1. **Buyer / design partner** — owns a decision and pays or receives the documented design-partner exception.
2. **Field contributors** — physically answer the question and earn only for accepted, settled evidence. Followers are irrelevant.
3. **Places / Field Station hosts** — may be the subject, destination, or permissioned distribution point. They are not automatically the buyer or a claimed partner.

Content creators who narrate the receipt are a later amplification layer. The first delivery requires reliable field contributors, not influencers.

## Readiness gate before money

- [ ] Legacy migration/schema drift reconciled.
- [ ] Required migrations applied to a disposable or preview PostgreSQL database from the real production baseline.
- [ ] Signed-wallet claim → trusted upload → proximity → reject/review → replacement → payout → receipt smoke passes.
- [ ] Production secrets, referee gas, USDC, and platform-wallet separation verified.
- [ ] Sponsor commercial-reuse rights remain `NOT_GRANTED` in the scope and receipt.
- [ ] One rollback owner and one pause condition are named.

Conversations, lead qualification, contributor screening, and Field Station permission can start before this gate. Funding and live launch cannot.

## The first buyer

### Fit criteria

Choose a buyer who:

- has one decision to make within 30 days;
- can ask an observable place question, not request vague “exposure”;
- will accept `NO`, `PARTIAL`, or `INCONCLUSIVE` as valid paid outcomes;
- can authorize the question and case-study receipt;
- can fund the $500 reward liability even if the service fee is waived;
- can decide `REPEAT`, `ADJUST`, `ASK`, or `STOP` after delivery.

Good first buyers: a small hostel group, travel newsletter, eSIM/mobility brand, coworking operator, local business group, or venue group. A single venue is valid only when it has a real decision question; it is not required.

### Question test

The question must fit this sentence:

> “By [date], we need to know whether [observable condition] is true at [specific place/area], because the answer changes [named decision].”

Example:

> “By next Friday, we need four independent checks of which public General Luna venues are genuinely suitable for a solo traveller to join between 7–9pm, including current entry conditions and visible price information, because it changes our guest recommendation sheet.”

Reject questions that ask BaseDare to guarantee traffic, purchases, virality, subjective popularity, covert filming, unsafe conduct, or a predetermined positive answer.

## The first contributor cell

Recruit **six people**: four assigned contributors plus two reserves.

Fit signals:

- physically present in the target micro-area for the delivery window;
- can follow a bounded brief and upload fresh evidence;
- comfortable receiving USDC on Base;
- no relationship that destroys the required independence;
- responsive enough to confirm or decline within 12 hours;
- understands that rejected evidence is not payable and one bounded replacement is allowed.

Followers, polished content, and a BareTag are not prerequisites for the first paid proof. Identity/reputation activation follows demonstrated value.

### Contributor truth

Say exactly:

> “This mission is funded at $125 gross. If your evidence is accepted and settles, you receive $120 and BaseDare receives the $5 settlement fee. A negative answer is payable when it honestly answers the brief. Acceptance is based on fresh, trusted, in-radius evidence—not whether the buyer likes the answer.”

Never promise instant approval, guaranteed payment for any upload, or that browser GPS is spoof-proof.

## 30-day execution

### Days 1–3 — prepare and qualify

Operator:

- close the release gate above;
- create one demo-safe receipt and one 90-second product walkthrough;
- make a list of 10 buyer candidates and rank by authority, question quality, urgency, and warm-intro strength;
- make a list of 15 contributor candidates and rank for reliability and location;
- prepare two permissioned Field Station hosts chosen by the founder.

Founder:

- request five warm buyer introductions;
- send five personalized direct messages;
- run three 15-minute question-discovery calls;
- do not pitch features before learning the buyer's decision.

Exit gate: one buyer can state the question, deadline, decision, authority, and budget owner.

### Days 4–7 — scope and close

- turn the buyer's language into one Field Truth question;
- show `YES / NO / PARTIAL / INCONCLUSIVE` examples;
- show the full $2,500 price before discussing the design-partner exception;
- record scope approval separately from payment confirmation;
- sign a short scope confirming question, area, freshness window, limitations, rights, reward liability, and repeat decision;
- collect/confirm the $500 reward pool and the service line or recorded exception;
- activate exactly two permissioned Field Stations only when their nearby inventory passes the density gate.

Exit gate: approved scope, confirmed funding lines, two station IDs, six screened contributors.

### Days 8–17 — deliver

- compile four immutable Field Truth contracts;
- create four real, non-simulated $125 escrows;
- assign four distinct contributors and retain two reserves;
- track routing → collection → review → settlement;
- deliberately preserve truthful negative or inconclusive outcomes;
- use the one bounded replacement only for an authoritative rejected/abandoned mission with separate replacement funding evidence;
- write accepted observations to timestamped place memory with refresh dates;
- keep precise contributor location and sensitive evidence private.

Exit gate: four accepted, settled outcomes from four distinct wallets and a conservative buyer receipt.

### Days 18–21 — close the loop

- deliver the receipt live on a 20-minute call;
- ask the buyer to select `REPEAT`, `ADJUST`, `ASK`, or `STOP` inside the receipt;
- capture what changed in their actual decision;
- ask permission for one narrow case study that respects the receipt's rights state;
- if `REPEAT` or `ADJUST`, scope Sprint #2 at the standard $2,500 price;
- if `STOP`, record why and do not spin the result as success.

### Days 22–30 — acquire from evidence

- publish one privacy-safe receipt story, not a generic launch announcement;
- ask each successful contributor for two reliable referrals;
- send the receipt to five look-alike buyers with one personalized sentence;
- convert the two Field Station host receipts into distribution conversations, not fake destination outcomes;
- sell only the same bounded SKU until the second paid receipt reveals a repeatable variation.

## Funnel targets

### Buyer funnel

`10 named prospects → 5 warm/direct conversations → 3 discovery calls → 1 approved question → 1 funded Sprint → 1 explicit repeat decision`

### Contributor funnel

`15 named prospects → 10 replies → 8 screened → 6 ready → 4 assigned → 4 settled outcomes → 2+ reliable repeat contributors`

### Field Station funnel

`2 permissioned hosts → 2 tested QRs → station entry → intent selected → target opened → Mission Pass saved → verified arrival`

Station scans and directions opens are interest, not foot traffic. Only accepted arrival/proof evidence advances the outcome ladder.

## Outreach copy

### Buyer opener

> Hey [name] — I’m testing BaseDare with one design partner. We take one real-world question your team actually needs answered, pay four independent local contributors to check it, verify the evidence, and return one timestamped receipt. The standard Sprint is $2,500: $2,000 managed delivery plus a separate $500 reward pool. For the first partner I can record a service-fee exception if the question is strong and you fund the contributor pool. What physical-world fact are you currently relying on that might be stale or wrong?

### Venue / place correction opener

> Hey [name] — your place is visible on the BaseDare map. I’m not claiming a partnership. I’d like to confirm the public details, preferred contact route, and one useful fact travellers often get wrong. Corrections are free; a paid verified field Sprint is separate if you later want a question independently checked.

### Contributor opener

> Hey [name] — I’m assembling a six-person local field cell for BaseDare. These are paid, bounded place checks—not influencer ads. The first Sprint has four independent missions funded at $125 gross each; an accepted settled answer pays $120 in USDC. A truthful “no” can still be the right answer. Are you around [micro-area] during [window] and comfortable following a precise evidence brief?

### Field Station host ask

> Can I place one small tracked BaseDare QR here for a two-week test? It gives guests a useful local answer before asking them to contribute. The QR does not prove a visit or claim a partnership. I’ll show you the station receipt—opens, actions, and verified outcomes kept separate—and remove it immediately if you want.

## Daily operator rhythm

- Morning: update 10 buyer and 15 contributor records; choose the next five human actions.
- Midday: two buyer asks, three contributor asks, one follow-up batch.
- Afternoon: product/release check, mission support, evidence review.
- Evening: record replies, objections, next steps, and one learning that changes tomorrow's message.
- Stop after one unanswered follow-up. No automated spam.

## Launch scorecard

Primary:

- approved buyer question;
- funded reward pool;
- four accepted settled outcomes;
- fully loaded verification cost;
- rejection/replacement rate;
- median verification time;
- explicit buyer repeat decision;
- Sprint #2 paid at $2,500.

Secondary:

- contributor reply-to-ready rate;
- repeat contributor count;
- station entry-to-action rate;
- Mission Pass recovery rate;
- privacy-safe receipt shares;
- organic useful actions per paid action.

Do not use raw scans, views, wallet connections, directions opens, seeded data, or sample records as customer traction.

## Human-only actions

- select and contact the design partner;
- approve outreach before sending;
- authorize invoices, exceptions, contracts, treasury, and escrow funding;
- approve venue/Field Station permission claims;
- review legal wording and sponsor commercial-reuse rights;
- choose whether the real receipt may become a public case study.
